Redesigning my universities website : A UI/UX case study

I redesigned my universities website’s user interface and user experience. It was my solo project.
I only worked on two pages as the website was pretty content heavy.

  • Role
  • UI/UX designer
  • Year
  • 2021

Overview

Durg University Or Hemchand Yadav Vishwavidyalaya is a official website of My University. With over 150k
students enrolled(Including me) and 138 Affiliated colleges, Its one of the most famous University in Our state.
From getting information about Syllabus to Exam Results to pretty much everything, We(students) use the Website regularly.

Problem and the goal

So after talking to my friends about what can be improved on this website and using this website personally(I mean, Obviously). I came across that these are the main problems we were facing and what I can do solve it.

  1. 1. The biggest problem was the visual noise that this website has. I wanted to improve the User interface by clearing this visual noise and giving it a clean and neat look.

  2. 2. The second problem was, Its Navigation. The Navigation bar was(in the original website) placed at the bottom of The Heading. Further more, the navigation bar was filled with repetitive links.

  3. 3. The biggest problem was the visual noise that this website has. I wanted to improve the User interface by clearing this visual noise and giving it a clean and neat look.

Current Landing Page of Durg University.

Target Audience

The target audience are obviously : The students. The main problem students were facing was 1. The visual noise that the website has and 2. The steps it took them to get their exam results. Personally I can tell that getting the result is pain in the current website.

Process

User Research

For the user research part I talked to my college friends via instagram DM’s and ask them what are the things that can be improved ?? After talking to them, these were the things that were the pain points they were facing while using the website:

    1. First problem was the Navigation. The navigation bar was on the bottom of the website’s name and the website logo(see the screenshot of website). This is where the mobile users were finding it difficult to navigate the website. Overall a bad navigation thus poor UX.

    2. Second problem the users were facing(the most painful one) was the steps it took the students(the users)to get their exam results. In the current website it takes 2–3 steps to get the exam results.

Wireframing

So after talking to one of my college friend(One of the users of the website) about the current website’s user interface and what can be improved in the website’s layout. I came up with these wireframes. While talking to my friend about the website, one thing that I’ve learned that: The website has so much visual noise and repetitive information’s. So while working on wireframes I had this my mind that the website’s UI has to be neat and clean.

Final wireframes for landing page and results page

Prototyping and Designing the UI

While working on the prototypes, I took everything that I’ve learned from wireframing. Like how I am gonna divide notifications and news sections that are really important for us students ?? The results page was the page that I think challenged me the most. I wanted the user(student) to get his/her result as quick as possible. I wanted the result form options to be as specific as possible. So after 2–3 iterations I Finally came up with the final prototype and the final UI screens. For the prototyping and designing the UI, I’ve used figma.

Hero Section of the Landing Page

Hero Section of the Landing Page: As you can see in the above image, Its the Hero section of the landing page. It is the section where the students will be able to login to their account or new students can create new account. Also this section have the some other info about the university like: How many students are enrolled so far, How many private colleges are affiliated with the university etc.

Quick Action and News-Notifications Section

Quick Action and News-Notifications Section: Here basically we have two sections. First Quick Action and the second is News and Notifications section. In the Quick Action section we have options where students would just go and get the information. In the News and Notification section, Students will get all the News and Notification that are necessary to know.

Quick Action and News-Notifications Section

Mailing list Section: In the mailing list section students can subscribe to the universities newsletter and Be updated whenever there’s new notifications.

Results Page

Results Page: On this page students will be able to get their exam results(I mean, Obviously). In order to get the exam results, Students will have to provide 5 necessary information’s. 1. The academic year they are studying in. 2. The course they are enrolled in. 3. Their student status, whether they are regular students or private students. 4. What type of exam it was, was it semester wise or yearly ? 5. This is pretty obvious, Their roll number. After submitting these information’s, Students will be able to get their exam results.

Mobile version of the Home page and Results page

Mobile version of Home page and Results page: Here we have nothing but the mobile versions of those two pages. Landing page and The results page.

Takeaways from this case study

This was my first time doing a UI/UX case study and Obviously I was excited to do this project and I am quite satisfied with what I’ve achieved with the project.

Apart from this I have learned the important of a good user experience and user research. I’ve learned that how user research is a backbone for a good UX. Not only that but A Good, Accessible, Clean and Neat User interface is also important for any digital product. A good UI will help the users to get their task done. The task, The main reason why they are using your product in the first place.
